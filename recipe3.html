Instructions for making the all-new Fried Chicken

Step 1: Prepare the Protein Base

• Mix pea protein isolate, soy protein isolate, and mycoprotein with binders (methylcellulose and egg whites/aquafaba) to form a smooth mixture.

• Add a small amount of olive oil or coconut oil for juiciness. Load this mixture into the 3D food printer.

Step 2: Set Printer Parameters

Adjust extrusion settings:

• Temperature: 60°C for maintaining structure.

• Nozzle Size: 1.5 mm for fibrous texture.

• Print Speed: 30 mm/s for smooth layering.

• Layer height: 0.7 mm for a smooth surface.

Step 3: Print the Chicken Base

• Use the printer to create cross-hatched layers to mimic muscle fibers.

• Print portions to the desired size (e.g., nuggets or drumstick shapes).

• Allow the printed pieces to set for a few minutes.

Step 4: Apply the Coating

• Dip the printed chicken pieces in a seasoned flour mix (oat flour, paprika, garlic powder, etc.), followed by panko breadcrumbs.

• Lightly spray with avocado oil for a crisp finish.

Step 5: Cook and Serve

• Air fry the coated pieces at 200°C for 10–15 minutes until golden and crispy.

• Serve hot with a side of your favorite dipping sauce.
