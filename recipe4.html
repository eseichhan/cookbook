2049 Cookbook

Instructions for the Dairy-Free Butter

Step 1: Ingredient Loading

The printer would have cartridges or compartments for each type of ingredient:

• Fats: Separate cartridges for coconut oil, olive/avocado oil, and flaxseed oil.

• Water: A cartridge for liquid storage.

• Powders: Compartments for lecithin, hydrocolloids (agar, xanthan), flavor molecules (diacetyl, lactic acid), and beta-carotene.

Step 2: Mixing Mechanism

The printer’s internal mixing unit would:

• Heat and melt solid fats like coconut oil.

• Combine fats, water, and emulsifiers to create a stable emulsion.

• Add hydrocolloids and stabilize the mixture for proper texture.

• Incorporate flavor and color molecules for a realistic buttery result.

Step 3: Layer-by-Layer Printing

Using its extrusion head, the printer would:

• Precisely deposit the butter mixture in layers.

• Use cooling mechanisms to allow the butter to solidify during the printing process.

• Create intricate designs or standard shapes (e.g., sticks, swirls, or patterns).

Step 4: Quality Control and Adjustments

The printer would have sensors to:

• Monitor viscosity, ensuring the mixture is suitable for extrusion.

• Adjust the temperature for fats and emulsions to maintain consistency.

• Ensure even deposition and prevent air bubbles.
